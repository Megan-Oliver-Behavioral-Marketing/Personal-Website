The Core Problem

Why Most Marketing Fails
at the Decision Point

Most campaigns fail at the decision point — not because the creative was bad or the targeting was off, but because they were built around who the audience is instead of what the audience is doing. Those are different problems with different solutions.

"The same person who ignores your message on Monday is your most receptive prospect on Thursday. Context changed. The strategy didn't."

Emotional marketing optimizes for how people feel about a brand. Behavioral marketing optimizes for the conditions that produce action. Both matter — but only one of them directly moves the business.

That distinction is where I work. And it changes every strategic decision: how you segment, how you sequence, what you measure, and where you spend.

Framework

Four Concepts That
Drive Behavioral Strategy

01
Decision-Making
People don't deliberate rationally before acting. They rely on shortcuts, patterns, and signals — understanding those patterns is the starting point for any effective strategy.
02
Friction
Every unnecessary step, unclear message, or moment of doubt between intent and action is friction. Behavioral strategy is about systematically removing it — or strategically adding it where you don't want action.
03
Incentives
What makes someone move isn't always what they say it is. Behavioral strategy surfaces the real incentives — status, certainty, ease, identity — that actually drive decisions.
04
Context
Context is not a trait of the person — it's a property of the environment. The same audience member in a different moment, channel, or headspace is a fundamentally different strategic target.
